Part A

How many hours of music will Casey play?

\[ \begin{aligned} MPC &= MPB \\ Q &= 80-Q &| &+Q \\ 2*Q &= 80 &| &\div 2 \end{aligned} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} Q = 40&&&&& \end{aligned} \]

Why?

\(MPC=MPB\) is where Casey’s cost for playing music is starts to outweigh her returns of doing so

On the Graph

Part B

What is the Marginal Social Cost (MSC)?

The social cost is the total cost involved, so private + external costs.

\[ \begin{aligned} MSC &= &MPC&+MEC \\ MSC &= &Q&+\frac{Q}{2.3} \end{aligned} \]

Part C

Calculate and indicate the pareto-efficient number of hours of music in the diagram.

First: The Result

How To Calculate That?

  • Pareto-efficient = No other better combination for everyone

  • In other words: \[ \begin{aligned} MSC &= MPB \\ MPC+MEC &= 80-Q \\ \end{aligned} \]

    \[ \begin{aligned} &&&&Q \approx 32.85 \end{aligned} \]

The Boring Calculation

\[ \begin{aligned} MSC &= MPB \\ Q+\frac{Q}{2.3} &= 80-Q &| +Q \\ 2Q+\frac{Q}{\frac{23}{10}} &= 80 \\ 2Q+\frac{10Q}{23} &= 80 \\ 2Q+\frac{10Q}{23} &= 80 &| *23 \\ 46Q+10Q &= 1840 \\ 56Q &= 1840 &| \div 56 \\ Q &\approx 32.85 \end{aligned} \]

Part C.b

  • What does it mean/imply that this quantity is pareto-efficient for this concrete case?

  • In this case, pareto-efficient means that there is no other way for both Casey and Jordan to be happier without decreasing the happiness of either.

Part D

What if Jordan is granted the property rights?

Part D.A

Initial quantity of played music?

  • Zero, because there is a cost involved of Casey even starting to play music

Part D.b

  • How would this result in pareto-efficiency?
  • Jordan can trade his tolerance for noise
  • Casey can compensate Jordan depending on how valuable playing music is to her

Note

This arrangement will always lead to the most efficient solution - if Casey and Jordan act ‘rational’.
(See Coase Theorem)

Part D.b

Does it matter who is granted the property rights?

  • The principles of the negotiation itself are not affected
  • Outcome is definitely changed

Note

Compare: Jordan compensating Casey vs. Casey compensating Jordan

  • Deciding initial property rights is an ethical question, e.g. “does Jordan deserve to endure noise pollution by default”
